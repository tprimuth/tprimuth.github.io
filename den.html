ABOUT

Sift is an app and desktop plug-in that assists with the userâ€™s hunt of finding clothing by universally filtering, using 3-Dimensional interactive models, emphasizing transparency, and saving products to organized hauls.

PROBLEM

Habitual shoppers across online platforms strive to find the perfect fashion piece. These shoppers are committed to hunting down a purchase they are proud of and confident in. Yet, the continual scrolling through endless products and comparison across brands becomes exhaustive, ultimately decimating the thrill of the shopping hunt.

SOLUTION

Sift aims to create a platform that increases the enjoyment and the efficiency of the fashion hunt while still inducing the feeling of finding the perfect product. Through the curation of the perfect amount of products, sift alleviates the exhaustion caused from browsing through excess products. All while keeping the shopping experience personal and inclusive for the differences between individuals.

Search

Users are able to set personalized filtering preferences that Sift applies across all brands, allowing users to compare fashion items more efficiently.

Save

Mimicking in-person clothing racks, users can swipe through an interactive 3-D view of products to accurately assess the form and quality of the product. Within the specific product pages, there are curated reviews, material comparisons and sizing assistance for a holistic understanding of the item before saving.

WHY SIFT?

The retail market is, and has been shifting towards being primarily online. As in-store shopping availability declines, there are critical experiences that are not being replicated in the shift to e-commerce. Right now, e-commerce fulfills shoppers utilitarian needs, yet overlooks the hedonic needs.

    Unique Selling Points

  • 3-Dimensional interaction to mimick physical shopping mental models
  • Personalized size assistant to help you find the perfect item
  • Customizable categories for your ideal product comparison
  • Sift's custom product compasison algorithm that filters the perfect amount for ease of searching
  • Save items to organized hauls to buy and refer back to later

Utilitarian Needs

Utilitarian shopping values are rational and task oriented, it can be considered a cognitive and non-emotional outcome of shopping. Utilitarian needs are fulfilled within a transactional service.

Hedonic Needs

Hedonic consumption satisfies emotional needs after basic needs have been met. These needs refer to the value perceived from the multisensory, atmospheric, and emotive aspects of the shopping experience.

On Boarding

Welcome screens introduce new users to Sift, its key offerings, and a basic understanding of the key elements.

3D Interactions

Using Maxon Cinema 4D I created a 3-dimensional clothing rack. This interaction allows the user to interact with Sift in a way that mimicks the in-person shopping experience. The rack is filled with the items that make up your filtered search. By using a 3-dimentional interaction Sift is able to tap into the hedonic needs when shoping online.

Size Assistant

The Sift size assistant is introduced in the initial on boarding. Users follow the measuring prompts on each screens and input their corresponding measurements. The size assistant is not only a way to mimic in person shopping interactions with a store associate but also allow Sift to better personalize your sifts based on your fit preferences. All measurements can be changed in the profile tab of the app.

Comparison Algorithm

To better personalize each individual users Sift we created a comparison algorithm for both product filtering and brand filtering. Each attribute is assigned a numerical value. Values are then compiled to create a string individual to the brand and product. Our AI technology compares and ranks numeric values. Results are displayed within Product Specific Page and Brand Transparency Characteristics.

Interface Design

The idea for the Sift interface was to design something that aligned with our 3-dimensional theme of mimicking real-world counterparts of in-store shopping in both how they appear and how the user interacts with them. To convey this feeling for the user we created a Skeuomorphism / Neomorphism interface. Skeuomorphism makes the interface objects familiar to users mental models by using concepts they recognize.
